Ebenezer: Thus far the LORD has helped us

Pastor Femi Olawale • Jun 13, 2022

A God to Pharaoh

The Philistines assembled against Israel with great intimidation of size and number and as expected, they were all fearful, except Prophet Samuel. The prophet engaged a none conventional weapon of war, he offered an unusual sacrifice to God: “And Samuel took a suckling lamb and offered it as a whole burnt offering to the LORD. Then Samuel cried out to the LORD for Israel, and the LORD answered him” (1Samuel 7:9). The heavens opened and thundered against opposition to confuse the Philistines and Israel defeated them.
Prophet Samuel saw the awesome manifestation of God and with a grateful heart sets up a stone and called it Ebenezer which implies “Thus far the LORD has helped us.”
Beloved, a thousand had fallen on your side and tens of thousand on your right side and you are still standing. You are a living witness of how death has dealt its blow on mankind, terminating the lives of many, yet you are still standing! It has pleased our Sovereign God; perfect and just in all His ways to allow some to be called to glory but He has chosen to preserve your life. Those who have been called to glory in Christ have been welcomed into the bosom of our Lord and Saviour.
You, however, joined the millions who cried upward to heaven for divine intervention over your life, family and nation and here you are, strong and healthy. Evidently it is time to set up your “stone of God’s help” and with a grateful heart to declare to all and sundry that “Thus far the LORD has helped us.”!
And the scripture said “So the Philistines were subdued, and they did not come anymore into the territory of Israel. And the hand of the LORD was against the Philistines all the days of Samuel.” God’s help translates to permanent victory when we learn to set up “stones of his help” to acknowledge his gracious manifestations in our lives.
TODAY, be that grateful soul who recognizes the mighty deliverance God has wrought in his life and set up your “stone” with a heart lifted up and a cry of EBENEZER to him who has done you well.
